About Team Water

What is Team Water

MrBeast and several global creators launched the #TeamWater initiative in partnership with WaterAid, aiming to raise $40 million by August 31, 2025 to provide clean drinking water to 2 million people across the world..

Our Mission

Team Water is a philanthropic initiative founded by YouTube superstar Mr. Beast (Jimmy Donaldson) with the mission to provide clean, safe drinking water to communities around the world that lack access to this basic human necessity. Recognizing that millions of people suffer from water scarcity and contaminated water sources, Team Water builds wells, installs purification systems, and implements sustainable water solutions in underserved regions.

Global Impact

Since its inception, Team Water has completed projects in over 15 countries across Africa, Asia, and South America. The initiative focuses on areas where water scarcity contributes to poverty, disease, and limited economic opportunities. Each project is carefully planned to ensure long-term sustainability, often involving local communities in maintenance training and governance of water resources.

Some Recent Projects

Malawi (Dyetse Village Africa)

Solar-powered water point installed in Dyetse Village to provide clean drinking water for local residents. Project included a newly drilled well or filtration point, combined with solar energy to run the pump/dispenser. WaterAid led construction and trained community members to maintain the system long-term. Part of broader infrastructure upgrades in rural Malawi supported by WaterAid’s local team.

Colombia ( La Guajira desert, South America)

A 10,000‑liter storage tank constructed to hold clean water. Pipeline network installed to deliver water to nearby settlements. Three-stage filtration system installed to remove dirt, salt, and bacteria—powered by solar panels. Local residents were trained to operate and maintain the system.

Nepal (Remote mountain Village, Asia)

Built three 15,000‑liter tanks on a hillside above 300+ villagers. Equipped each tank with disinfection/filtration mechanisms to ensure safe drinking water. Delivered water closer to homes—cutting the need to fetch from distant or unsafe sources. Project filming by the Stokes Twins highlighted long journeys residents made previously.

Ecuador (River-based community, South America)

Implemented reverse-osmosis filtration units to purify contaminated river water. Installed systems linked to village water storage tanks. Distributed portable filtration bags for personal use to ensure safe water access on the go. Done in collaboration with creator partners and community groups.

Zambia (Large rural community, Africa)

Community of ~9,000 people had relied on shallow, polluted wells. #TeamWater installed three 10,000‑liter tanks, supported by water-access stations. Clean water is now reliably available without walking miles or using unsafe sources. Local community-led operation ensures sustainability and training by WaterAid.
